Policy Without Evidence: A Risk to Sustainable Development

Policies built on assumptions rather than evidence often fail — not because of intent, but because of weak foundations.

In many contexts, policies are designed under pressure — political, financial, or institutional. This often leads to decisions being made without sufficient evidence.

The Risk

When policy is not grounded in data, it can lead to ineffective interventions, wasted resources, and unintended consequences.

Building Evidence into Policy

  • Use evaluation findings in design stages
  • Integrate feedback mechanisms
  • Test assumptions before scaling

Evidence does not slow policy — it strengthens it.

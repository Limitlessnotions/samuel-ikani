The Problem with Vanity Metrics

Many organizations measure activity, not impact. This creates the illusion of progress without real outcomes.

Vanity metrics are indicators that look impressive but do not reflect meaningful change. They are easy to track and easy to report — but rarely useful.

Why They Persist

Organizations often prioritize metrics that are easy to collect rather than those that are meaningful. This leads to a focus on outputs instead of outcomes.

Examples of Vanity Metrics

  • Number of participants trained
  • Number of workshops conducted
  • Reports produced

What Actually Matters

Impact-focused systems prioritize outcomes such as behavior change, improved systems, and measurable results.

The goal is not to measure more — but to measure what matters.
